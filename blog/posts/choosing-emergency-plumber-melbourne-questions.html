Choosing an Emergency Plumber in Melbourne: 7 Questions to Ask

Not all 'emergency' plumbers turn up fast or quote fairly. Before you hire, ask these 7 critical questions to protect yourself from cowboys, hidden fees, and shoddy workmanship.

26 May 2026 7 min read Consumer Advice

Why asking the right questions matters

When water is flooding your kitchen or sewage is backing up into your bathroom, it's tempting to hire the first plumber who answers the phone. But not all emergency plumbers are equal — and making the wrong choice can cost you thousands in inflated bills, substandard repairs, and repeat call-outs.

In Melbourne's unregulated emergency trade market, anyone with a van and a phone can claim to be a 24/7 plumber. The difference between a qualified professional and a cowboy operator often comes down to what you ask before they arrive.

These 7 questions separate legitimate, licensed Melbourne emergency plumbers from those who'll leave you worse off. Print them. Save them. Use them every time.

1. Are you licensed by the Victorian Building Authority (VBA)?

This is the single most important question. In Victoria, all plumbing work must be performed by a licensed plumber registered with the Victorian Building Authority (VBA). Unlicensed work is illegal, voids your home insurance, and can leave you liable for property damage or injuries.

A legitimate plumber will gladly provide their VBA licence number. Verify it immediately at vba.vic.gov.au using the Practitioner Search tool. Don't take their word for it — check it yourself.

Red flag: Any hesitation to provide a licence number, vague claims of being 'qualified' without specifying VBA registration, or suggestions that licensing 'doesn't matter for small jobs'.

2. Are you actually available 24/7, or just answering phones?

Here's a dirty secret: many plumbers advertise '24/7 emergency service' but only operate an after-hours answering service. When you call at 2am, you get a friendly receptionist who takes your details — then tells you a plumber will arrive 'first thing in the morning.'

That's not an emergency plumber. That's a booking service dressed up as 24/7.

Ask specifically: "Is there a licensed plumber available to come to my location right now?" True 24/7 operations have plumbers on rotating call, ready to dispatch immediately. They can give you an estimated time of arrival (ETA) during the call, not tomorrow morning.

3. How fast can you arrive at my Melbourne suburb?

Response time varies dramatically based on where the plumber is coming from and current workload. A plumber based in Frankston will struggle to reach Footscray quickly, even if they're genuinely available.

Ask for a specific ETA to your suburb. Quality emergency plumbers in Melbourne typically quote:

  • Inner suburbs: 45–70 minutes
  • Outer suburbs: 60–90 minutes

Be wary of plumbers who won't commit to an arrival window or give vague promises like 'we'll be there soon.' Professional operations track their vans via GPS and can give you realistic ETAs.

4. Do you charge a call-out fee — and what does it include?

Call-out fees are standard in emergency plumbing, but what's included varies wildly. Some plumbers charge $150–$350 simply to knock on your door — before they've touched a tool. Others roll the call-out fee into the total job cost if you proceed with the repair.

Ask specifically:

  • "What is your call-out fee?"
  • "Is it separate from labour charges?"
  • "If I go ahead with the repair, is the call-out fee waived or discounted?"

Get this in writing if possible, or at least confirmed clearly over the phone. Vague answers about 'assessment fees' often translate to surprise charges on your invoice.

5. Is your pricing fixed upfront, or charged by the hour?

This question reveals whether you're dealing with a professional or a potential scammer. Reputable emergency plumbers provide fixed, upfront pricing — or at least a clear price range — before starting work.

Hourly billing incentivises slow work. A plumber charging by the hour has no reason to work efficiently. Fixed pricing aligns their interests with yours: get the job done right, quickly, with no surprises.

Ask: "What will this job cost? Is that a fixed price or an estimate?" If they can't give you a ballpark figure after describing the problem, or if they refuse to commit to any pricing discussion until arrival, proceed with extreme caution.

6. Do you guarantee your work?

Professional plumbers stand behind their work. Ask about warranties on both labour and parts:

  • Labour guarantee: How long is the workmanship covered? (Quality plumbers typically offer 6–12 months)
  • Parts warranty: Are manufacturer warranties honoured? Will they handle warranty claims if a part fails?
  • Repeat visits: If the same problem recurs, do they return free of charge?

Plumbers who won't guarantee their work or provide vague assurances ('we'll look after you') are essentially admitting their repairs might not last. Get specific timeframes in writing.

7. Are you local to my Melbourne suburb?

Local knowledge matters in emergency plumbing. A plumber familiar with your suburb understands:

  • Common pipe materials and age in your area (clay, copper, PVC, galvanised)
  • Local water pressure characteristics
  • Heritage building plumbing requirements (critical in suburbs like Fitzroy, South Melbourne, Williamstown)
  • Fastest routes to your location during Melbourne's unpredictable traffic

While not every job requires hyper-local expertise, it helps — especially in Melbourne's older suburbs with original 19th and early 20th-century plumbing infrastructure.

Ask where they're based and whether they regularly service your suburb. Be cautious of plumbers who won't disclose their location or claim to 'cover all of Melbourne' from a single base — they may be subcontracting to whoever is available, with variable quality.

FAQ: Hiring emergency plumbers in Melbourne

Are emergency plumbers required to be licensed in Melbourne?

Yes. All plumbers working in Victoria must hold a valid licence from the Victorian Building Authority (VBA). You can verify any plumber's licence at vba.vic.gov.au using their licence number. Never hire an unlicensed plumber — it voids insurance coverage and puts your property at risk.

Do all emergency plumbers in Melbourne actually operate 24/7?

No. Many plumbers advertise '24/7' but only offer after-hours answering services, with actual plumbers unavailable until morning. A true 24/7 emergency plumber has licensed plumbers on call and can dispatch to your location immediately, any time of day or night. Always ask: 'Is there a plumber available to come right now?'

What response time should I expect from an emergency plumber in Melbourne?

Response times vary by location and time of day. In inner Melbourne suburbs, expect 45–70 minutes. Outer suburbs may take 60–90 minutes. Ask for an estimated time of arrival (ETA) when you call, and confirm whether the plumber is already in your area or travelling from across town.

How do I know if an emergency plumber will charge fair upfront pricing?

A reputable plumber provides fixed pricing or a clear price range before starting work. Ask: 'What will this cost?' and 'Is this a fixed price or an hourly rate?' Avoid plumbers who refuse to give estimates, charge by the minute, or won't put pricing in writing. Get an itemised quote covering labour, parts, and any call-out fees.

What's the risk of hiring an unlicensed plumber in Melbourne?

Unlicensed plumbing work is illegal in Victoria and automatically voids your home and contents insurance. If the unlicensed work causes flooding, gas leaks, or property damage, you're personally liable for repair costs and any injury claims. Additionally, you'll need to pay a licensed plumber to redo the work to code before selling your property.

Should I get multiple quotes for emergency plumbing?

For true emergencies (burst pipes, gas leaks, sewage backups), speed matters more than shopping around. You don't have time for three quotes. That's why asking the 7 questions above on your first call is critical — it helps you identify a qualified, fairly priced plumber immediately. For non-urgent work, getting 2–3 quotes is sensible.
