How to Turn Off Your Water Mains in an Emergency

When a pipe bursts or a major leak starts, shutting off the water main is the single most important thing you can do. Find your stop tap now — before you ever need it in a Melbourne emergency.

19 May 2026 7 min read Burst pipes

Answer first: Locate your stop tap or water meter shut-off valve, turn it clockwise (gate valve) or 90° perpendicular to the pipe (ball valve), then confirm by opening a cold tap inside. If water keeps flowing, use the street-side meter valve or call a 24/7 emergency plumber immediately.

Every Melbourne homeowner should know where their water main shut-off is — yet most don't until water is already flooding the laundry. A burst pipe can release over 400 litres per hour. The difference between a minor mop-up and thousands of dollars in damage often comes down to finding and closing that valve within the first two minutes.

This guide covers where to look in typical Melbourne properties, how house and street stop taps differ, the exact shut-off steps, and what to do in apartments with shared meters.

Where is my water main in Melbourne?

Melbourne's housing stock spans 19th-century terraces, post-war bungalows, modern estates and high-rise apartments — and the water main location varies with each. Check these spots in order:

  • Front boundary — water meter box

    The most common location in standalone houses across Melbourne suburbs. Look for a rectangular cover at the footpath or front fence line, often marked with your water retailer's logo (e.g. Yarra Valley Water, South East Water, Greater Western Water).

  • Near the hot water unit

    Laundry, garage or dedicated hot water cupboard. A brass or chrome tap on the incoming mains pipe, sometimes labelled "main stop tap" or "mains isolation".

  • Under the kitchen sink

    Common in inner-city Fitzroy, Carlton and South Melbourne terraces where the front meter is inaccessible or the mains enters through the rear laneway.

  • Apartment services cupboard

    High-rise and medium-density buildings often have a shut-off inside the unit, on the floor's common services landing, or in the basement plant room — check your building's emergency handbook.

Pro tip: Walk your property this weekend and locate your stop tap. Take a photo on your phone and label it "water main". In an emergency, you won't be searching blind.

House stop tap vs street stop tap

Most Melbourne properties have two potential shut-off points. Understanding the difference helps when one fails or is hard to reach.

Type Location When to use Notes
House / internal stop tap Laundry, garage, under sink First choice in emergencies Fastest access; shuts water to whole house
Street / meter stop tap Front boundary meter box If internal tap fails or unknown May need a meter key; can be stiff or buried
Apartment isolation valve Unit, floor cupboard, basement Apartment leaks only May affect neighbours if on shared riser

The house stop tap sits on the pipe after it enters your property — it isolates your home without affecting neighbours. The street stop tap is at or near the water meter on the supply side; it's your backup if the internal tap is seized, hidden behind built-in cabinetry, or you simply can't find it under stress.

In Victoria, homeowners are generally permitted to operate their own property shut-off valve in an emergency. If the street valve is corroded, missing or requires a special key, call your water retailer or a licensed emergency plumber rather than forcing it.

Step-by-step: how to shut off your water main

Follow these steps in order when facing a burst pipe, major leak or overflowing fixture:

  1. 1
    Locate the valve

    Go to your known stop tap location — front meter box, hot water cupboard, or under the kitchen sink. Use a torch if needed.

  2. 2
    Identify the valve type

    Gate valve: round wheel handle — turn clockwise. Ball valve: lever handle — rotate 90° until perpendicular to the pipe.

  3. 3
    Turn off firmly

    Gate valve: rotate clockwise until you feel resistance — do not over-tighten a stiff valve; use a spanner on the nut behind the wheel if it won't budge by hand. Ball valve: push or pull the lever to the closed position.

  4. 4
    Confirm water is off

    Open a cold tap at the lowest point in the house. Flow should stop within 30–60 seconds. If water continues, try the street-side meter valve or call for help.

  5. 5
    Call a 24/7 emergency plumber

    With water stopped, call a licensed Melbourne plumber for permanent repair. See our full burst pipe guide: What to do when a pipe bursts.

Never restore the mains until the burst or leak has been professionally repaired. Turning water back on to a split pipe will restart flooding instantly.

Apartments and shared meters

Apartment and townhouse plumbing in Melbourne adds complexity — you may not control the only shut-off point, and closing the wrong valve can cut water to neighbouring units.

Where to look in an apartment

  • Inside your unit: Under the kitchen sink, laundry tub, or bathroom vanity — look for a small chrome or brass isolation tap on the incoming cold water line.
  • Common services cupboard: Many Melbourne apartment buildings have a floor-level cupboard (often locked) with isolation valves for each unit. Your building manager or strata plan should document locations.
  • Basement plant room: In larger complexes, main isolation may be in a basement services area — only building management or emergency services should operate these unless you have explicit access.

Shared meters and strata properties

Some Melbourne townhouses and older converted buildings share a single water meter across multiple dwellings. In these cases:

  • Each unit should still have its own internal isolation tap — find yours before an emergency.
  • Shutting the shared street meter affects all connected properties — notify neighbours or the body corporate if you must use it.
  • If your internal tap fails and you share a meter, call the building manager and a 24/7 emergency plumber simultaneously.

What if the valve is seized or missing?

Older Melbourne homes — particularly pre-1970s properties in Footscray, Brunswick and Williamstown — often have corroded gate valves that won't turn. Do not force a seized valve with excessive leverage; you can snap the spindle or crack the body, making the leak worse.

Options when the internal tap won't move:

  • Try the street-side meter valve with a meter key or adjustable spanner.
  • Call a 24/7 emergency plumber — they carry tools to isolate mains under pressure safely.
  • Contact your water retailer for a meter shut-off in extreme cases where both valves have failed.

FAQ: Turning off water mains in Melbourne

Where is the water main in a Melbourne home?

Most commonly at the front boundary near the water meter, near the hot water unit in the laundry or garage, or under the kitchen sink in older inner-city terraces. Apartments may have a shut-off inside the unit or in a common services cupboard — check your building's emergency information.

Which way do I turn a stop tap to shut off water?

Gate valves (round wheel handles) turn clockwise to close — 'righty tighty'. Ball valves with a lever handle rotate 90 degrees until the lever is perpendicular to the pipe. Never force a seized valve; use a spanner on the nut behind the wheel or call a plumber.

Can I turn off water at the street meter in Melbourne?

Yes, if your internal stop tap fails or you cannot locate it. The street-side shut-off is usually in the meter box at your front boundary. In Victoria, you are generally permitted to operate your own property shut-off valve in an emergency. If the valve is seized or missing, call your water retailer or a 24/7 emergency plumber.

How do I turn off water in a Melbourne apartment?

Look for an isolation tap under the kitchen sink, in the laundry, or in the bathroom vanity. If not found, check the common services cupboard on your floor or contact the building manager for the main isolation valve location. Never shut off a shared riser without notifying neighbours if it affects their supply.

How often should I test my stop tap?

Test your stop tap every six months: turn it off, confirm taps run dry, then turn it back on. Regular exercise prevents valves from seizing — a common problem in Melbourne's older homes where taps haven't been moved in decades.

What do I do after shutting off the water main?

Drain remaining water by opening cold taps at the lowest point, contain any flooding, document damage with photos for insurance, and call a licensed 24/7 emergency plumber for permanent repair. Read our full guide: What to do when a pipe bursts.
