Emergency Guide

What to Do When a Pipe Bursts (Before the Plumber Arrives)

A burst pipe can release hundreds of litres per hour. The first 15 minutes matter: shut off water, stay safe around electricity, drain the system, contain damage — then call a licensed 24/7 emergency plumber in Melbourne.

31 Mar 2026 6 min read Burst pipes

Answer first: Turn off your water main immediately, then manage electrical safety, drain taps to relieve pressure, contain and photograph the damage, and call a licensed emergency plumber. Do not wait — every minute of flowing water increases repair and insurance costs.

Whether it's a frozen copper line in a Carlton terrace, a corroded galvanised pipe in a Williamstown cottage, or a failed joint in a South Melbourne apartment stack, burst pipes in Melbourne homes follow the same urgent response pattern. What you do in the first few minutes often determines how much damage — and how much your insurance claim — costs.

This guide walks through the five steps in order. Print it, save it on your phone, and know where your stop tap is before you ever need it.

Turn off the water main

Stopping the water supply is always step one. Every second the main stays open, pressurised water continues feeding the burst — through walls, under floorboards, into cupboards and electrical cavities.

Find your stop tap. In most Melbourne homes, the main shut-off is at one of these locations:

  • Front boundary / water meter: A tap or valve on the pipe entering your property — common in standalone houses and many suburban blocks.
  • Near the hot water unit: Some homes have an internal mains tap in the laundry, garage or hot water cupboard.
  • Under the kitchen sink: Older inner-city terraces sometimes have a mains isolation tap beneath the sink.

Turn the valve clockwise until it stops. If it's a lever-style ball valve, rotate it 90° so it sits perpendicular to the pipe. Test that water has stopped by opening a tap — it should trickle to nothing within a minute.

Switch off electricity if needed

Water and electricity are a lethal combination. If the burst pipe is flooding near power points, appliances, light fittings or your switchboard, you need to cut power — but only if you can do so safely.

Safe to switch off: Water is pooling on the floor but has not reached the switchboard, and you can reach the main switch without stepping in water deeper than ankle height. Turn off the main switch at your switchboard.

Do NOT switch off if: Water has reached or is spraying toward the switchboard, you would need to stand in deep water to reach it, or you see sparking or smell burning. In these cases, stay clear, evacuate the affected area, and call 000 if there is immediate danger — then call an emergency electrician.

Unplug nearby appliances (fridge, washing machine, dishwasher) if you can reach them without standing in water. Do not touch anything plugged in while standing on a wet floor.

Drain the taps

Even with the mains off, water remains trapped in your pipes under pressure. Draining the system reduces stress on the burst section and slows residual leaking.

  1. 1
    Open cold taps

    Start at the lowest tap in the house — laundry tub, ground-floor bathroom or outdoor tap. Work upward.

  2. 2
    Flush toilets

    Empty the cistern and bowl to drain the branch lines connected to your sewer stack.

  3. 3
    Leave taps open

    Keep them open until the plumber arrives so any remaining pressure can escape safely.

Do not drain your hot water system unless the burst is on the hot water line or the unit itself is leaking — isolating hot water requires a separate valve, usually marked on the unit or nearby pipework.

Contain and document the damage

Once water is stopped and power is safe, shift focus to limiting damage and building your insurance record.

Contain the water

  • Move furniture, rugs, electronics and valuables out of the affected area.
  • Use towels, buckets and mops to soak up standing water — start from the highest point and work down.
  • Place aluminium foil or plastic under furniture legs to prevent staining on wet carpets.
  • Open windows and run fans if safe, to start drying walls and subfloors.

Document everything (before major cleanup)

Insurance claims live or die on evidence. Before you strip wet carpet or pull damaged plasterboard:

  • Take photos and video of every affected room, including ceilings, skirting boards and cupboards.
  • Photograph the burst pipe location if visible — do not dismantle anything the plumber needs to inspect.
  • Record the date, time, and what you observed (water flow rate, areas affected).
  • Keep damaged items unless your insurer instructs otherwise — they may need to inspect them.

When to call an emergency plumber

Call a licensed 24/7 emergency plumber immediately after shutting off the water main — do not wait until morning, even if you've contained the visible flooding.

Burst pipes are true emergencies because:

  • Hidden water can soak into wall cavities, subfloors and structural timber for hours.
  • Mould can begin forming within 24–48 hours in Melbourne's humid conditions.
  • Temporary DIY patches on pressurised pipes almost always fail when the mains is restored.
  • Insurance policies often require prompt professional mitigation — delaying the call can affect your claim.

FAQ: Burst pipes in Melbourne

What is the first thing to do when a pipe bursts?

Turn off the water main immediately. Locate your stop tap or water meter shut-off valve and close it clockwise. Stopping the water supply limits flooding and reduces damage while you wait for an emergency plumber.

Should I turn off electricity if a pipe bursts?

Yes, if water is near power points, appliances or your switchboard. Turn off power at the main switch only if you can reach it safely without standing in water. If water has reached the switchboard, stay clear and call a licensed electrician.

Can I fix a burst pipe myself?

Temporary containment with towels and buckets is fine, but permanent pipe repair requires a licensed VBA plumber. DIY patches on pressurised pipes often fail and can void insurance. Call a 24/7 emergency plumber for a proper fix.

How fast should I call an emergency plumber for a burst pipe?

Call immediately after shutting off the water main. Burst pipes are true emergencies — even with the water off, hidden leaks can damage walls, floors and foundations. Melbourne emergency plumbers typically arrive within 50–80 minutes depending on suburb.

Where is the water main in a Melbourne home?

Most commonly at the front boundary near the water meter, or near the hot water unit in the laundry or garage. Inner-city terraces may have a tap under the kitchen sink. See our step-by-step guide: How to turn off your water mains.

Will insurance cover burst pipe damage in Melbourne?

Sudden and accidental burst pipe damage is usually covered under home and contents policies, but gradual leaks often are not. Document everything with photos before cleanup and call your insurer promptly. Read our burst pipe insurance guide for the full checklist.
