Burst Pipe Water Damage: What Your Insurance Needs to Know

A burst pipe can flood your Melbourne home in minutes — and the insurance claim that follows depends heavily on what you document in the first hour. Here's what's typically covered, what insurers reject, and the checklist that protects your claim.

2 Jun 2026 10 min read Burst pipe · Insurance · Melbourne

Answer first: Most Melbourne home insurance policies cover sudden and accidental burst pipe water damage — but not gradual leaks, poor maintenance or damage you failed to mitigate. Shut off the water, call a licensed emergency plumber, photograph everything before cleanup, notify your insurer promptly, and request a written plumber's report. How you act in the first 60 minutes often determines whether your claim succeeds.

Every year, Melbourne emergency plumbers attend hundreds of burst pipe call-outs — under floorboards in South Melbourne terraces, in apartment stacks in the CBD, and through ageing galvanised lines in western suburbs cottages. The plumbing repair is only half the story. Water damage to floors, walls, cabinetry and personal belongings can cost $5,000–$50,000+ to restore.

Insurance can cover much of that cost — if you understand what your policy requires and document the damage correctly from the start. This guide walks Melbourne homeowners through coverage rules, the documentation checklist insurers expect, the critical sudden vs gradual damage distinction, your legal mitigation duty, and how to use your plumber's report to support your claim.

Is burst pipe water damage covered by insurance?

Most standard Australian home building and contents policies cover water damage from a sudden and accidental burst pipe — but coverage varies significantly between insurers and policy tiers. Here's what is typically included and excluded:

Scenario Usually covered? Notes
Sudden pipe burst — internal pipe Yes Building + contents damage typically covered
Burst pipe under floorboards / in wall Yes Includes strip-out, drying, restoration costs
Appliance hose failure (washing machine, dishwasher) Yes Sudden failure; document hose age if known
Storm-related roof leak Often yes Sudden storm damage covered; maintenance exclusions apply
Gradual leak over weeks/months No Slow drip, rusted pipe, known unrepaired leak
Pipe failure due to lack of maintenance No Corroded pipes you knew were failing
Flood from external source (river, flash flood) Often no May require separate flood cover — check PDS
Damage you failed to mitigate Reduced / denied Leaving water running overnight without calling a plumber

Important: This is general guidance only — not legal or financial advice. Always read your Product Disclosure Statement (PDS) and call your insurer or broker to confirm coverage for your specific policy before assuming a claim will be accepted.

Building vs contents: Building insurance covers the structure — walls, floors, fixed cabinetry, pipes. Contents insurance covers movable belongings — furniture, electronics, clothing, rugs. Many Melbourne homeowners hold both under a combined policy. Damage to each category is claimed separately with its own excess.

Document everything — insurance claim checklist

Insurers assess claims based on evidence. The single biggest mistake Melbourne homeowners make is cleaning up before documenting. Follow this checklist in order — ideally while waiting for your emergency plumber to arrive:

  • Photograph and video before cleanup

    Capture all affected rooms from multiple angles. Include wide shots and close-ups of water depth, damaged walls, ceilings, floors, cabinetry and belongings. Enable timestamp on your phone camera if possible.

  • Document the burst location

    Photo the burst pipe, failed joint or appliance connection before the plumber repairs it. This is critical evidence for sudden vs gradual determinations.

  • Create a written inventory of damaged items

    List every damaged belonging with approximate age, purchase price if known, and replacement estimate. Include brand, model and serial numbers for electronics and appliances.

  • Keep all receipts

    Emergency plumbing call-out, after-hours fees, drying equipment hire, temporary accommodation, storage costs, and any emergency purchases ( buckets, fans, dehumidifiers).

  • Notify your insurer promptly

    Most policies require notification within 24–72 hours. Call your insurer's claims line as soon as the immediate emergency is under control. Get a claim reference number.

  • Request a plumber's written report

    Ask your licensed plumber for a report describing the cause of the burst, location, pipe type and age, repair work performed, and whether any pre-existing deterioration was observed.

  • Do not dispose of damaged items immediately

    Insurers may want to inspect damaged belongings. Store salvageable items; photograph and bag unsalvageable items until your insurer advises otherwise.

  • Log a timeline

    Note when you discovered the burst, when you shut off water, when the plumber arrived, and when you notified your insurer. Timestamps strengthen your claim.

Gradual vs sudden damage — why insurers reject claims

The most contested area in water damage claims is whether the damage was sudden and accidental or gradual. Understanding this distinction before you call your insurer helps you describe the event accurately and set realistic expectations.

Factor Sudden & accidental (usually covered) Gradual (usually excluded)
How it happened Pipe burst without warning; immediate flooding Slow drip over weeks; rust hole developed over time
Homeowner awareness No prior signs; discovered during or immediately after burst Stain visible for months; known leak not repaired
Pipe condition Unexpected failure of sound pipe or fitting Corroded, aged pipe that failed predictably
Damage pattern Localised flooding from single failure point Spreading mould, rot, stain growth over time
Plumber's assessment "Catastrophic failure — no prior deterioration observed" "Long-term corrosion / pre-existing leak path identified"
Typical Melbourne example Flexi hose under sink bursts at 2am Galvanised pipe in 1960s Footscray home slowly weeps for months

Insurers often send an assessor or request your plumber's report to determine which category applies. A licensed plumber's professional opinion on whether the failure was sudden or showed pre-existing deterioration carries significant weight in claim decisions.

Your mitigation duty — what insurers expect you to do

Every Australian home insurance policy includes a duty to mitigate — a legal obligation to take reasonable steps to prevent further damage after an insured event. Failing this duty can reduce your payout or void your claim entirely, even if the original burst was covered.

What reasonable mitigation looks like

  • Shut off the water main immediately — see how to find your stop tap
  • Call a licensed emergency plumber promptly — do not wait until morning on active flooding
  • Contain water with buckets, towels and plastic sheeting where safe
  • Move valuables away from affected areas
  • Arrange drying if advised by your plumber or insurer — mould develops within 24–48 hours in Melbourne's humid conditions
  • Do not delay notification to your insurer while waiting for a perfect documentation set

What counts as failure to mitigate

  • Leaving a burst pipe running overnight to avoid after-hours plumbing fees
  • Knowing about a slow leak for weeks and not repairing it before major damage occurs
  • Refusing to allow drying equipment or restoration contractors access after the insurer arranges it
  • Attempting DIY repairs that worsen the damage or conceal the cause

Working with your plumber's report for insurance

A detailed report from a licensed VBA plumber is one of the most valuable documents in a burst pipe insurance claim. Insurers, assessors and loss adjusters rely on it to determine cause, coverage and repair scope.

What to ask your plumber to include

  • Date, time and property address

    Official header with plumber's VBA licence number and business details.

  • Description of the emergency call-out

    What you reported, what the plumber found on arrival, and water damage observed.

  • Cause and location of the burst

    Specific pipe, joint or appliance; pipe material and approximate age if determinable.

  • Whether failure was sudden or showed pre-existing deterioration

    Critical for sudden vs gradual insurance determinations.

  • Repair work performed

    Parts replaced, pipe sections renewed, compliance certificate issued if applicable.

  • Photographs taken by the plumber

    Before and after repair — many Melbourne emergency plumbers include these in their reports.

  • Itemised invoice

    Separate emergency call-out, labour, parts and any after-hours premium — attach to the report.

FAQ: Burst pipe water damage and insurance in Melbourne

Does home insurance cover burst pipe water damage in Melbourne?

Most home and contents policies cover sudden and accidental burst pipe damage — including structural damage and ruined belongings. Gradual leaks, poor maintenance and failure to mitigate are commonly excluded. Check your PDS and notify your insurer promptly after a burst.

What should I document for a burst pipe insurance claim?

Photograph and video all damage before cleanup, document the burst location, create an inventory of damaged belongings, keep all receipts, notify your insurer within 24–72 hours, and request a written report from your licensed plumber.

What is the difference between sudden and gradual water damage?

Sudden and accidental damage — like an unexpected pipe burst — is typically covered. Gradual damage from a slow leak over time, rusted pipes or known unrepaired problems is usually excluded. Your plumber's report helps insurers determine which applies.

Should I claim burst pipe damage on insurance or pay myself?

Compare your excess against the total repair and restoration cost. For major flooding ($3,000+), claiming usually makes sense. For minor damage below your excess, paying directly may be simpler. See our emergency plumber cost guide for typical repair ranges.

Will my premiums increase after a burst pipe claim?

Possibly — insurers may adjust premiums or impose excess changes after a claim, though burst pipe claims are generally viewed differently to repeat preventable claims. Ask your insurer about no-claim bonus impact before lodging. This varies by provider and claim history.

Can I use any plumber for an insurance claim in Victoria?

No. All plumbing work must be performed by a VBA-licensed plumber. Unlicensed repairs void insurance and are illegal. Ask for the licence number before work begins and verify at vba.vic.gov.au. Read our 7 questions to ask an emergency plumber.
