Warning signs

Signs you have a burst or leaking pipe

Some bursts are obvious — others start as hidden leaks that cause major damage before you notice. Watch for these signs:

  • Active water spraying or flooding

    Water visibly escaping from walls, ceilings, under sinks or through floorboards — true emergency, call immediately.

  • Sudden loss of water pressure

    Weak flow from all taps may indicate a major supply line break you have not yet located.

  • Unexpectedly high water bill

    A sharp increase without changed usage often signals a hidden leak under the property or in walls.

  • Damp patches on walls or ceilings

    Growing wet spots, peeling paint or bubbling plaster — especially after no recent rain.

  • Hissing or rushing water sounds

    Audible water movement inside walls or under floors when no taps are running.

  • Water near electrical fittings

    Any flooding near power points or the switchboard — treat as electrical emergency too.

Local context

Common causes of burst pipes in Melbourne homes

Melbourne's ageing housing stock, variable water pressure and seasonal temperature swings create predictable burst pipe patterns across the city.

  • Corroded galvanised pipes — pre-1970 homes in Footscray, Brunswick, North Melbourne and inner-west cottages. Rust weakens pipe walls until they fail under pressure.
  • Failed flexi hoses — braided hoses under sinks, toilets and washing machines have a 5–10 year lifespan. Sudden catastrophic failure is common.
  • Freeze-thaw stress — rare but real in Melbourne hill suburbs (Olinda, Mount Dandenong fringe) and uninsulated external pipes on cold winter nights.
  • High water pressure — pressure above 500 kPa stresses joints and fittings. Common in hills suburbs and properties without a pressure limiting valve.
  • Tree root pressure — roots wrap and crush external supply lines, especially near large eucalypts in eastern garden suburbs.
  • Renovation damage — nail or drill penetration of concealed pipes during DIY or tradie work in terrace renovations across Fitzroy and Carlton.
  • Pipe age and joint failure — copper pinhole leaks and failed solder joints in 1980s–2000s builds across outer Melbourne suburbs.
Our methods

How we repair burst and leaking pipes

Permanent repair to Australian plumbing standards — not a temporary wrap that fails under pressure overnight.

Electronic leak detection

For hidden bursts — damp patches without an obvious source — we use acoustic and thermal leak detection to pinpoint the failure before cutting walls or floors. Saves unnecessary damage and speeds repair in Melbourne terraces and apartments where pipes run through shared cavities.

Emergency pipe repair and replacement

Once located, we isolate the damaged section, remove failed pipework and install new copper, PEX or appropriate material to code. Accessible bursts (under sinks, at the hot water unit, external taps) are often repaired within 1–2 hours. Concealed bursts may require controlled access through walls or floorboards with restoration advice provided.

Insurance documentation and reports

We photograph the burst location before repair, provide itemised invoices and written plumber's reports describing cause, pipe type and work performed — everything Melbourne insurers expect for sudden water damage claims. Read our burst pipe insurance guide.

Act now

Emergency burst pipe? What to do right now

Every minute the water main stays open, damage spreads. Follow these steps while our plumber is dispatched.

  1. Turn off the water main — clockwise at the stop tap or meter valve. See how to find your water main in Melbourne.
  2. Switch off electricity if water is near power points or the switchboard — only if safe to reach without standing in water.
  3. Drain taps at the lowest point in the house to relieve pipe pressure.
  4. Contain and photograph — buckets, towels, move valuables. Photo everything before cleanup for insurance.
  5. Call [TELEFONE] — 24/7 permanent repair, not a temporary patch.

FAQ — burst pipe plumber Melbourne

How much does burst pipe repair cost in Melbourne?

Accessible bursts typically cost $250–$500 during business hours. Concealed bursts requiring leak detection and wall access range from $500–$2,000+. After-hours emergencies add a premium. We quote upfront before starting.

Is a burst pipe a plumbing emergency?

Yes — always, if water is actively flowing. A burst can release 400+ litres per hour. Shut off the main and call immediately. See what counts as a plumbing emergency.

Will insurance cover burst pipe damage?

Most policies cover sudden and accidental burst pipe damage. Document with photos before cleanup, mitigate further loss, and request our written plumber's report. See our insurance guide.

Can I patch a burst pipe myself?

Temporary containment with buckets is fine, but permanent repair requires a licensed VBA plumber. DIY patches on pressurised pipes fail under pressure and can void insurance claims.

Where is my water main in Melbourne?

Usually at the front boundary near the water meter, near the hot water unit, or under the kitchen sink. Apartments may have an isolation valve at the entry point. Full guide: how to turn off your water mains.
