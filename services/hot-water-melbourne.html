Warning signs

Signs your hot water system needs attention

Some symptoms mean a simple repair — others mean replace immediately before the tank floods your laundry.

  • No hot water or lukewarm water

    Completely cold or inconsistent temperature from hot taps — failed element, thermostat or gas valve.

  • Rusty or discoloured hot water

    Brown or yellow water from hot taps only — internal tank corrosion, especially in units over 10 years old.

  • Banging, rumbling or popping noises

    Sediment buildup at the tank base causing overheating and noise — common in hard water areas.

  • Water pooling at the base of the unit

    Tank has corroded through — replace immediately, not repair. True emergency if actively leaking.

  • Gas smell near a gas hot water unit

    Evacuate and call a licensed gas fitter — see our gas leak guide. Do not relight the pilot yourself.

  • Unit age over 10–12 years

    Electric storage tanks typically fail at 10–12 years in Melbourne. Plan replacement before emergency failure.

Local context

Common hot water problems in Melbourne homes

Melbourne's water quality, climate and housing stock create predictable hot water failure patterns by system type and suburb.

  • Failed heating element or thermostat — most common electric storage repair. Units in apartments across the CBD and inner city.
  • Extinguished pilot light or thermocouple failure — gas storage units in terraces and cottages throughout North Melbourne and Fitzroy.
  • Pressure relief valve (PRV) discharge — continuous dripping from the relief line indicates over-pressure or valve failure.
  • Sacrificial anode depletion — accelerates tank corrosion in Melbourne's moderately hard water areas. Anode replacement extends tank life.
  • Heat pump compressor or fan failure — growing issue as heat pump adoption increases across Victoria post-rebate programs.
  • Gas continuous flow — insufficient flow rate — multiple hot taps running simultaneously exceed unit capacity in large family homes.
  • Scale buildup in older units — reduces efficiency and causes rumbling in eastern and northern suburbs with higher mineral content.
Our methods

How we repair and replace hot water systems

We diagnose first, then recommend repair or replace — honest advice based on unit age, condition and your household needs.

Electric storage hot water

We test elements, thermostats and tempering valves, replace faulty parts, and flush sediment from older tanks. If the tank itself is leaking from the base, replacement is the only safe option. Common in Melbourne apartments and older suburban homes.

Gas storage and continuous flow

Licensed gas fitters diagnose pilot, thermocouple, gas valve and burner issues on storage units. Continuous flow systems are checked for gas supply, flow rate adequacy and heat exchanger condition. All gas work includes compliance certification where required.

Heat pump hot water systems

We service and install heat pump systems — the fastest-growing category in Melbourne due to VIC rebates and lower running costs. Compressor, fan and refrigerant issues diagnosed on site. We advise on Solar Victoria and STC rebates for eligible replacements.

Act now

Emergency hot water? What to do right now

Response depends on whether you have no hot water, an active leak, or a gas smell — each needs a different first step.

  • No hot water only (no leak, no gas smell)

    Check circuit breaker (electric) or gas supply to other appliances (gas). Book same-day service — urgent but not a midnight emergency unless vulnerable household members are affected.

  • Active leak at tank base or relief valve

    Turn off water supply to the unit at the isolation valve. Place buckets. Call [TELEFONE] immediately — 24/7 emergency.

  • Gas smell near hot water unit

    Do not relight pilot. Evacuate, ventilate and call a licensed gas fitter. See gas leak warning signs.

Good to know

FAQ — hot water plumber Melbourne

How much does hot water repair cost in Melbourne?

Part replacements (elements, thermostats, gas valves) typically cost $180–$400. Full system replacement ranges from $1,200 for electric storage to $5,500 for heat pump (before rebates). We quote upfront before starting.

Can you replace my hot water system same day?

Yes — like-for-like replacements are often completed same day, especially if you call before midday. We carry common units for popular Melbourne configurations. Switching system types may take a full day.

Should I repair or replace my hot water system?

Repair if under 8 years old with a replaceable part fault. Replace if the tank is leaking from the base, over 10–12 years old, or repair costs exceed 50% of a new unit. See our failing hot water guide.

Is no hot water an emergency?

Not usually — unless the unit is actively leaking, you smell gas, or household members depend on hot water for health. Same-day booking is recommended. Active tank leaks and gas smells require immediate 24/7 response.

What hot water rebates are available in Victoria?

Eligible heat pump and solar hot water replacements may qualify for Solar Victoria rebates, VEU incentives and federal STCs. We apply available rebates to your quote. See our system types comparison.
