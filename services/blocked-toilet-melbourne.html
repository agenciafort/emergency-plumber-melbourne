Warning signs

Signs you have a blocked or failing toilet

Some toilet problems are a quick fix — others signal a main sewer line emergency affecting your whole home.

  • Bowl filling and overflowing

    Water rising to the rim or spilling onto the floor after a flush — stop water immediately and call if it won't stop.

  • Won't flush or slow to drain

    Water level stays high after flushing — partial blockage in the trap or drain line.

  • Constantly running cistern

    Water running into the bowl after the tank fills — worn flapper, faulty inlet valve or float mechanism.

  • Sewage smell from the toilet

    Indicates a partial blockage, dry trap, or venting issue — worsens if sewage backs up.

  • Multiple fixtures affected

    Shower, sink and toilet all draining slowly or backing up — main sewer line blockage, not just the toilet.

  • Gurgling from other drains when toilet flushes

    Air displaced by a partial sewer blockage — early warning before full backup.

Local context

Common causes of blocked toilets in Melbourne homes

From inner-city apartments to suburban family homes — these are the blockages Melbourne plumbers see every day.

  • Flushed wet wipes and sanitary products — the leading cause of single-toilet blockages despite "flushable" labelling.
  • Excessive toilet paper — especially low-flow toilets in Melbourne apartments and newer builds.
  • Foreign objects — children's toys, dental floss, cotton buds and hygiene products dropped in the bowl.
  • Main sewer line blockage — tree roots in clay pipes across eastern garden suburbs; affects all fixtures simultaneously.
  • Faulty cistern components — worn flapper valves and inlet valves causing constant running and eventual overflow.
  • Old low-flow or siphon-jet design — common in 1990s–2000s Melbourne homes with weak flush capacity.
  • Shared stack blockages — multi-storey apartments in the CBD and Southbank where one unit's blockage affects neighbours.
Our methods

How we fix blocked and overflowing toilets

We diagnose whether the blockage is in the toilet trap, branch line or main sewer — then clear it permanently.

Toilet plunger and drain auger clearing

For single-fixture blockages confined to the toilet trap or close drain line, we use professional-grade plungers and closet augers (toilet snakes) to dislodge paper, wipes and soft obstructions without damaging the porcelain bowl or wax seal.

High-pressure jet blasting for sewer lines

When multiple fixtures are affected or the blockage is in the main sewer line, we deploy jet blasting and CCTV inspection from an external access point — the same equipment used for blocked drain clearing. Common when tree roots have infiltrated clay pipes.

Cistern repair and toilet replacement

Running toilets, faulty flush buttons, leaking base seals and cracked cisterns — we carry common parts on the van for same-visit repair. Full toilet replacement when the unit is beyond economical repair or during bathroom renovations.

Act now

Emergency overflowing toilet? What to do right now

The first 60 seconds limit damage and contamination — especially in Melbourne homes with timber bathroom floors.

  1. Lift the cistern float — remove the lid, raise the float arm or press the shut-off button to stop water entering the bowl.
  2. Turn off the isolation tap — small tap behind the toilet base, turn clockwise until tight.
  3. Do not flush again — this is the most common mistake that turns a small overflow into a flood.
  4. Contain with towels and buckets — keep sewage off other surfaces. Wear gloves if handling contaminated water.
  5. Call [TELEFONE] if overflow continues, sewage is on the floor, or other drains are gurgling or backing up.

Good to know

FAQ — blocked toilet plumber Melbourne

How much does it cost to unblock a toilet in Melbourne?

Simple unblocking typically costs $150–$280. Main sewer line blockages $350–$800+. Running toilet repairs $120–$250. We quote upfront before starting.

Is an overflowing toilet an emergency?

Yes, if overflow won't stop or sewage is on the floor — raw sewage is a health hazard. A single contained overflow after isolating the cistern may wait until business hours. See what counts as a plumbing emergency.

Why does my toilet overflow when I flush?

Usually a blockage in the trap or drain line preventing water from exiting. If other fixtures are also slow, the main sewer line is likely blocked — possibly tree roots. See our tree roots in pipes guide.

Can I use a plunger to unblock my toilet?

Yes, for a simple single-toilet blockage with no overflow — use a flange plunger with a firm seal. Do not plunge if sewage has already spilled, or if multiple fixtures are affected. Call a plumber instead.

How do I stop a running toilet?

Turn off the isolation tap behind the toilet to stop water waste immediately. Running toilets are usually caused by a worn flapper or faulty inlet valve — inexpensive parts we carry on the van.
