Warning signs

Signs you have a roof leak or blocked gutter problem

Roof and stormwater issues often hide until Melbourne's first heavy downpour. Watch for these warning signs before and during storm season.

  • Brown water stains on ceilings

    Discoloured patches that grow after rain — often near skylights, valleys or external walls where flashings meet the roof.

  • Active dripping during storms

    Water entering through light fittings, cornices or upper walls only when it rains heavily — a true emergency roof plumbing call.

  • Peeling paint or bubbling plaster

    Moisture trapped in ceiling cavities causes paint to bubble and plaster to soften — common in period Melbourne homes.

  • Overflowing gutters in light rain

    Gutters that spill over during moderate rain are partially blocked — they will fail completely in a Melbourne storm cell.

  • Water cascading over fascia boards

    Blocked downpipes or full gutters force water behind fascia and into eaves — often the first visible sign from street level.

  • Musty smell in upper rooms after rain

    Hidden moisture in roof spaces or wall cavities — mould risk increases rapidly if not addressed before the next downpour.

Local context

Common roof and stormwater problems in Melbourne homes

Melbourne's sudden storm cells, ageing housing stock and mature tree canopy create predictable roof plumbing failure patterns across the city.

  • Blocked gutters from eucalyptus debris — Melbourne's gum trees drop leaves and bark year-round. Gutters in Kew, Camberwell, Hawthorn and inner suburbs clog fast without regular clearing.
  • Ageing terracotta tiles and perished pointing — period homes in East Melbourne, North Melbourne and Fitzroy have original or decades-old roof coverings that crack under storm pressure.
  • Failed valley flashings — galvanised valleys corrode over 30–50 years. Wind-driven rain penetrates weakened seams during heavy downpours.
  • Box gutter overflow on flat-roof extensions — common on Melbourne post-war and modern extensions where internal box gutters block and overflow into ceiling spaces.
  • Stormwater connection blockages — downpipes connected to underground stormwater drains can block with sediment, roots and debris — backing water up into gutters.
  • Skylight and flashing failures — silicone seals degrade in UV. Skylights on north-facing Melbourne roofs fail predictably after 10–15 years.
  • Leaf guard traps — poorly installed gutter guards that trap debris rather than shed it — worse than no guard at all during storm season.

Act now

Roof leaking in a storm? What to do right now

Active roof leaks during Melbourne storms need immediate containment — then a licensed emergency roof plumber on site.

  1. Stay safe around electricity — turn off power to affected circuits at the switchboard if you can reach it safely without standing in water.
  2. Contain active drips — buckets under leaks, tarps over furniture, move valuables away from the water path.
  3. Do not go on the roof — wet roofs are lethal in wind and rain. Wait for licensed roof plumbers with proper safety equipment.
  4. Check downpipe outlets from ground level — obvious blockages at the base can sometimes be cleared safely without climbing.
