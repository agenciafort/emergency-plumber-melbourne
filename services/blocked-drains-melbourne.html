Warning signs

Signs you have a blocked drain

Catch a blockage early — before it becomes a sewage backup or a burst pipe from pressure buildup.

  • Water draining slowly

    Sink, shower or basin takes longer than usual to empty — often the first sign of a partial blockage.

  • Gurgling sounds

    Air trapped in pipes escapes through fixtures — common when a sewer line is partially blocked.

  • Water backing up

    Water rising in a shower when the toilet flushes, or vice versa — indicates a shared drain blockage.

  • Sewage smell indoors

    Odour from floor drains, bathrooms or laundry — health hazard requiring immediate 24/7 response.

  • Multiple fixtures blocked

    More than one drain affected simultaneously — main sewer line blockage, not a single trap.

  • Recurring blockages

    Same drain blocking every few months — likely tree roots in pipes. See our tree roots guide.

Local context

Common causes of blocked drains in Melbourne homes

Melbourne's mix of Victorian-era clay pipes, 1960s galvanised lines and modern PVC creates distinct blockage patterns by suburb and building age.

  • Tree roots in clay sewer pipes — endemic in garden suburbs like Camberwell, Hawthorn and Brighton. Roots enter through joints and grow inside the pipe.
  • Grease and fat buildup — kitchen sinks in restaurants and homes along Lygon Street, Chapel Street and suburban shopping strips.
  • Hair and soap scum — bathroom drains in apartments across the CBD and inner city.
  • Flushed wet wipes and sanitary products — even "flushable" wipes cause blockages in Melbourne sewer systems.
  • Collapsed or offset pipes — ground movement and age in North Melbourne cottages and Footscray workers' cottages.
  • Stormwater debris — leaves and sediment in grated drains after Melbourne autumn storms.
Our methods

How we clear blocked drains

We match the clearing method to your pipe type, blockage location and severity — permanent fix, not a quick poke.

CCTV drain camera inspection

A waterproof camera is fed into your pipework to locate the exact blockage point, identify pipe condition (cracks, root intrusion, collapsed sections) and confirm the clearing method before we start. Essential for recurring blockages and pre-purchase inspections.

High-pressure water jet blasting

Our primary clearing method for Melbourne sewer and stormwater lines. Pressurised water (up to 5,000 PSI) cuts through grease, tree roots, scale and debris — cleaning pipe walls, not just punching a hole through the blockage. Safe for PVC, clay and cast iron when used by trained operators.

Electric eel (drain snake) clearing

A flexible steel cable with cutting head for smaller blockages in accessible traps, u-bends and short runs. Effective for hair clogs, object obstructions and initial clearing before jet blasting on tougher blockages.

Act now

Emergency blocked drain? What to do right now

If water or sewage is overflowing, follow these steps while our plumber is on the way.

  1. Stop using all plumbing fixtures if sewage is present or multiple drains are blocked.
  2. Do not flush toilets connected to a blocked line — this worsens overflow.
  3. Contain water with towels and buckets. Move valuables and keep children and pets away from sewage.
  4. Safe DIY only on single fixtures: a plunger on a sink or toilet may clear a simple trap blockage. Do not force chemical drain cleaners into blocked lines.

FAQ — blocked drain plumber Melbourne

How much does it cost to clear a blocked drain in Melbourne?

Single-fixture blockages typically cost $200–$350 during business hours. Main sewer line clearing with CCTV and jet blasting ranges from $350–$800+. After-hours emergencies add a premium. We quote upfront before starting.

How do you clear blocked drains?

High-pressure water jet blasting is our primary method for sewer and stormwater lines. CCTV camera inspection locates the blockage. Electric eel clearing handles smaller accessible blockages. We choose the method based on pipe type and blockage severity.

Is a blocked drain an emergency?

Yes if water or sewage is overflowing, multiple fixtures are blocked, or you smell sewage indoors. A slow single fixture can usually wait until business hours. See what counts as a plumbing emergency.

What causes blocked drains in Melbourne?

Tree roots in clay pipes, grease buildup, hair, flushed wet wipes, and collapsed ageing pipes. Recurring blockages often indicate tree roots — see our tree roots in pipes guide.

Can I use chemical drain cleaner?

Occasional use on a slow single fixture may help, but repeated use damages older Melbourne pipes and creates chemical hazards for plumbers. Professional jet clearing is safer and more effective for stubborn blockages.
