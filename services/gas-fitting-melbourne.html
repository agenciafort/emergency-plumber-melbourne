Warning signs

Signs you have a gas leak or gas fitting problem

Natural gas is odourless — suppliers add a rotten egg smell so leaks are detectable. Never ignore these warning signs.

  • Rotten egg or sulphur smell

    Near gas meter, cooktop, hot water unit, heater or along pipe runs — strongest when appliances are running.

  • Hissing or whistling sound

    Pressurised gas escaping from meter, regulator, flexible hose or pipe joint.

  • Headache, dizziness or nausea indoors

    Multiple household members affected simultaneously — evacuate immediately.

  • Yellow or orange burner flames

    Gas burners should burn blue. Discoloured flames indicate incomplete combustion — call a gas fitter.

  • Dead vegetation above gas line

    Underground leak killing grass or plants in a line above the service pipe.

  • Unexpected gas bill increase

    Sudden rise without changed usage — possible slow leak.

Local context

Common gas fitting issues in Melbourne homes

Melbourne's established gas network, period housing and renovation culture create predictable gas safety patterns across the city.

  • Perished flexible appliance hoses — cooktop and bayonet hoses have a 5–10 year lifespan. Sudden failure is common in older inner-city kitchens.
  • Loose connections after renovation — vibration and movement in terrace renovations across Fitzroy, Carlton and Richmond loosen fittings over time.
  • Faulty hot water gas valves — pilot, thermocouple and gas valve failures on ageing storage units in suburban homes.
  • Uncapped gas lines after appliance removal — illegal and dangerous DIY during kitchen renovations — requires licensed capping and certification.
  • Corroded external pipework — pre-1980 steel pipes in western suburbs cottages and north-side workers' dwellings.
  • Incorrect DIY gas work — unlicensed connections void insurance and fail compliance checks at sale or rental inspection.
  • Carbon monoxide from faulty heaters — unflued or poorly maintained gas heaters in Melbourne period homes — annual servicing essential.

Act now

Emergency gas leak? What to do right now

Gas leaks are always true emergencies. Follow these steps before our gas fitter arrives.

  1. Do not create ignition sources — no matches, switches, appliances or phones used inside the gas-affected area.
  2. Turn off gas at the meter if safe — lever perpendicular to the pipe at your front boundary meter box.
  3. Evacuate and ventilate — open doors on your way out, move everyone upwind to fresh air.
  4. Call from outside — 000 for fire/explosion/medical emergency · 1800 427 532 (Australian Gas Networks, metro Melbourne) · then [TELEFONE] for repair.
  5. Do not re-enter until our licensed gas fitter confirms the system is safe.
